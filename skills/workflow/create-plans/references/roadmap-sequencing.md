# Roadmap Sequencing Notes

Use these notes when a plan starts from a roadmap or release sequence instead of a single project brief.

## Focus

- keep the release or quarter structure visible
- preserve sequencing rationale, not just the item list
- call out dependencies and enabling work explicitly
- separate committed work from exploratory or low-confidence work

## Useful Structure

For each release or planning slice, capture:

- the business or customer outcome
- the initiatives included
- the dependencies and prerequisites
- the success signals
- the main risk or assumption

## Guardrails

- do not flatten a roadmap into an unordered backlog
- do not present low-confidence items as commitments
- do not lose the strategic through-line when decomposing work
