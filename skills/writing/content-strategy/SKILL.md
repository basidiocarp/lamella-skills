# Content Strategy

Use this skill to plan what content should exist before drafting individual pieces. The goal is to connect product context, customer questions, search demand, and audience behavior into a content plan that has a clear reason to exist.

## When to Use

- Building a new content program from scratch
- Resetting a blog or content calendar that feels scattered
- Choosing content pillars and topic clusters
- Mapping topics to awareness, consideration, decision, or implementation stages
- Deciding whether a topic should be searchable, shareable, or both

## Boundaries

- Use `writing/content-writer` when the strategy is set and the task is to draft a specific asset.
- Use `agile-pm/plan-discovery-interviews` or `agile-pm/discover-interview-synthesis` when the real gap is customer evidence.
- Use `agile-pm/discover-company-profile` when the work is a single-company research brief rather than a publishing strategy.

## Workflow

1. **Define the business goal**
   State whether the content is meant to drive traffic, leads, authority, launch support, customer education, or a mix of those.

2. **Ground the plan in real inputs**
   Gather:
   - customer questions
   - sales objections
   - support patterns
   - product strengths
   - competitive gaps

3. **Choose the content pillars**
   Define three to five pillars that are broad enough to support repeated publishing but narrow enough to connect back to product value.

4. **Sort topics by intent**
   Decide whether each topic is:
   - searchable
   - shareable
   - both

   Use `references/content-strategy-reference.md` when the distinction is fuzzy.

5. **Map topics by buyer stage**
   Build clusters that cover awareness, consideration, decision, and implementation. Make sure the plan does not over-index on one stage while neglecting the rest of the journey.

6. **Prioritize the first batch**
   End with a first publishing slate that names:
   - the topic
   - the intended audience
   - the content type
   - the buyer stage
   - why this topic matters now

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- the business goal and audience
- the content pillars and rationale
- the topic clusters
- the searchable versus shareable mix
- the buyer-stage map
- the recommended first batch of topics

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Pillars connect to real customer questions or product value
- [ ] Topic choices are not just keyword chasing without strategic fit
- [ ] Searchable and shareable work are both considered deliberately
- [ ] Buyer-stage coverage is visible instead of implied
- [ ] The first batch is small enough to execute
