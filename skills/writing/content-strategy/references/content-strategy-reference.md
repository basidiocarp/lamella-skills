# Content Strategy Reference

Use this reference when planning topic mix, cluster structure, and buyer-stage coverage.

## Searchable Versus Shareable

- `Searchable`: captures existing demand from people actively looking for an answer
- `Shareable`: creates demand through insight, opinion, story, or novelty
- `Both`: strongest when a topic has clear search intent and a differentiated point of view

## Content Types

| Type | Best fit | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Use-case article | Searchable | Works well for persona plus problem framing |
| Hub and spoke | Searchable | Good for major themes with multiple layered subtopics |
| Template or checklist | Searchable and implementation-stage | Useful when the product helps the user act on the template |
| Thought leadership | Shareable | Needs a real point of view, not generic hot takes |
| Data or research | Shareable and searchable | Strong when the insight is unique and reusable |
| Case study | Decision-stage | Best when it shows proof, not just praise |

## Buyer-Stage Map

| Stage | Typical user question | Useful content shape |
|-------|-----------------------|----------------------|
| Awareness | What is this problem? | definitions, guides, explainers |
| Consideration | Which approach or tool is best? | comparisons, alternatives, tradeoff pieces |
| Decision | Should I choose this? | pricing, proof, case studies, rollout detail |
| Implementation | How do I make it work? | templates, setup guides, how-to content |

## Prioritization Questions

Score candidate topics against:

1. customer evidence
2. product relevance
3. business timing
4. differentiation potential
5. effort to produce

Prioritize topics that are strong on evidence and relevance before chasing volume alone.
