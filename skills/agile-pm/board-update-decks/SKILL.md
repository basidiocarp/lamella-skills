# Board Update Decks

Use this skill to turn company updates into a decision-ready deck. The goal is not to list activity. The goal is to show the current state, explain the important variance, and end with clear asks.

## When to Use

- Preparing a quarterly board deck or monthly investor update
- Reworking a leadership deck that has too much data and no through-line
- Framing a difficult update where bad news needs to be surfaced cleanly
- Building a repeatable template for executive reporting

## Workflow

1. **Confirm the audience and cadence**
   Decide whether the deck is for a formal board meeting, a lightweight investor update, or a fundraising process. Tailor depth and tone to that context.

2. **Collect the smallest useful metric set**
   Prefer 6-8 metrics with targets, prior-period comparison, and status. If a number is unknown, mark it `[TBD]`. Do not invent values.

3. **Build the story before polishing slides**
   Use this sequence:
   - Executive summary
   - Key metrics
   - Financial and commercial performance
   - Product, delivery, and team updates
   - Risks
   - Strategic outlook and asks

4. **Explain variance directly**
   For every material miss or surprise, state:
   - what happened
   - why it happened
   - what is changing next

5. **End with board-level decisions or asks**
   Be specific. Ask for introductions, approvals, feedback, or decisions with clear owners and timing.

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- a recommended slide order
- the headline for each slide
- the key data required for each section
- the 2-4 concrete asks at the end of the deck

Use the template in `references/board-deck-template.md` when the user needs a fill-in-ready draft. Use `references/deck-frameworks.md` when the user needs to adapt the structure for board, investor, or fundraising contexts. Use `references/narrative-frameworks.md` when the deck needs tighter audience framing or bad-news delivery.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The executive summary says what changed and what matters next
- [ ] Every core metric includes a target or comparison point
- [ ] Bad news is stated plainly instead of buried
- [ ] Product and engineering updates are tied to user or business impact
- [ ] The final slide contains specific asks, not vague requests
