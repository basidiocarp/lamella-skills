# Director Transition Coaching

Use this skill to coach a product leader through the shift from strong PM execution to Director-level leverage. The focus is on scope, delegation, strategic narrative, people leadership, and early-role recalibration.

## When to Use

- A senior PM wants to understand the real gap between PM and Director
- A product leader needs interview preparation for Director roles
- A newly promoted Director keeps defaulting to tactical rescue work
- A Director has been in role for a while and something is not clicking

## Boundaries

- Use `collaboration/founder-leadership-coaching` for founder-specific delegation, calendar, and energy coaching.
- Use `agile-pm/company-operating-rhythm` for company cadence, scorecards, and accountability design.
- Use `agile-pm/organizational-health-diagnostic` for business or org health reviews instead of individual transition coaching.

## Workflow

1. **Locate the transition stage**
   Place the person in one of four states:
   - preparing for the move
   - actively interviewing
   - newly landed
   - recalibrating after time in role

2. **Name the real leadership gap**
   Diagnose the main shift that is still missing:
   - thinking at higher altitude
   - coaching through other PMs
   - creating strategic narrative
   - navigating peers and executives
   - letting go of hero behavior

3. **Match the advice to the stage**
   Keep the guidance stage-specific. Interview prep, early-month transition, and later-role repair should not be mixed together.

4. **Translate the gap into behavior changes**
   Convert the diagnosis into specific practices such as delegation moves, 1:1 reframes, story preparation, context-cascade exercises, or stakeholder habits.

5. **End with a near-term plan**
   Give the next two to four concrete moves for the next month, not generic leadership encouragement.

Use `references/conversation-flow.md` when the user needs a worked example of the coaching structure.

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- the transition-stage diagnosis
- the main readiness or recalibration gap
- the highest-leverage behavior changes
- the next-month practice plan
- the main failure mode to watch

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The advice is matched to the user's transition stage
- [ ] Recommendations focus on leverage, not personal heroics
- [ ] The plan includes observable behavior changes
- [ ] Interview guidance is framed in Director-level language
- [ ] The final output ends with a short execution plan
