# Define Pricing Strategy

Use this skill to design or reset pricing for a software product. The goal is to make pricing match customer value, support the right buying motion, and create a defensible path for packaging, page design, and price changes.

## When to Use

- Defining pricing for a new product or major packaging reset
- Auditing pricing that feels misaligned with customer value
- Choosing between flat, seat-based, usage-based, or hybrid pricing
- Designing tiers, plan boundaries, and enterprise packaging
- Planning a price increase without treating it as a pure finance exercise

## Boundaries

- Use `agile-pm/discover-competitive-analysis` when the question is mainly about competitor position and market messaging.
- Use `agile-pm/evaluate-feature-investment` when the question is whether one feature deserves investment.
- Use `agile-pm/deliver-launch-checklist` after the pricing decision is made and the team needs launch readiness.

## Workflow

1. **Define the pricing problem**
   State whether the work is:
   - net-new pricing
   - packaging redesign
   - pricing-page audit
   - price increase planning

2. **Capture the value and segment logic**
   Clarify:
   - target customer segments
   - the value drivers customers actually pay for
   - the current or intended buying motion
   - the next-best alternative customers compare against

3. **Choose the pricing structure**
   Decide the right mix of:
   - value metric
   - packaging
   - price point
   - enterprise boundary

   Use `references/pricing-models.md` when the model choice is the hard part.

4. **Design the tiers**
   Define the entry, middle, and top plans. Make the middle plan the default for the intended growth segment, and keep the top tier focused on high-value control, compliance, and support needs.

5. **Pressure-test the page and rollout**
   If the work includes a pricing page, use `references/pricing-page-playbook.md` to check page structure, CTA clarity, and trust signals. If the work is a price increase, define who is affected, what changes for new customers versus existing customers, and what message makes the increase defensible.

6. **Finish with a pricing recommendation**
   End with a direct recommendation covering:
   - pricing model
   - tier structure
   - price logic
   - rollout or testing notes

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- the pricing problem statement
- the customer and value logic
- the proposed model and value metric
- the tier structure and packaging rules
- the pricing-page or rollout guidance
- the final recommendation and open risks

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The pricing model matches how value scales for the customer
- [ ] Tier boundaries are clear and segment-specific
- [ ] Packaging and price point are not treated as the same decision
- [ ] Recommendations separate verified evidence from pricing judgment
- [ ] The final output is concrete enough to implement or test
