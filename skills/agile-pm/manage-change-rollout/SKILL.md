# Manage Change Rollout

Use this skill to turn a change decision into a rollout plan that people can understand, adopt, and sustain. The emphasis is on awareness, stakeholder impact, enablement, and reinforcement.

## When to Use

- Rolling out a new tool or process
- Announcing a reorganization or reporting-line change
- Communicating a strategy pivot or product shutdown
- Reinforcing a behavior or culture change across teams

## Workflow

1. **Define the change and the reason**
   State the problem being solved, why the change is happening now, and what happens if nothing changes.

2. **Map stakeholder impact**
   Separate what changes for individual contributors, managers, and leadership. Be explicit about what becomes easier, harder, or different.

3. **Build the rollout around ADKAR**
   Cover:
   - awareness
   - desire
   - knowledge
   - ability
   - reinforcement

4. **Write the communication plan**
   Include:
   - message owner
   - audience
   - timeline
   - support channel
   - FAQ themes

5. **Plan reinforcement before launch**
   Decide how adoption will be measured, where the old path is removed, and how lagging adoption will be handled.

Use `references/change-playbook.md` when the user needs a deeper rollout sequence for tool migrations, reorganizations, pivots, or culture changes. Use `references/crisis-communication.md` when the change has urgent internal communication or rumor-control risk.

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- a one-page change brief
- the stakeholder-specific messaging plan
- a rollout timeline
- enablement and support steps
- reinforcement and adoption checks

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The rationale is honest and concrete
- [ ] Stakeholder concerns are addressed directly
- [ ] Training, documentation, and support are assigned to named owners
- [ ] Adoption is measured after launch
- [ ] The old way is deprecated or constrained where appropriate
