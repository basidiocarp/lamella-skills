# Crisis Communication

Use this reference when a change or incident needs fast internal communication before rumors or external messaging take over.

## Core Rule

Employees should hear material company news from leadership first. If the event is already public or likely to become public, communicate internally within hours, not days.

## Minimum Sequence

1. **State what happened**
   Use direct language and avoid hedging.

2. **State what is known and unknown**
   Separate confirmed facts from open questions.

3. **State what is being done**
   Give the immediate response and who owns it.

4. **State what people should do**
   Clarify whether anyone needs to change customer communication, pause work, or route questions to a specific owner.

5. **State the next update time**
   Give a concrete time for the next communication.

## Internal Message Skeleton

```markdown
What happened:
[Short factual description]

What we know:
- [Fact]
- [Fact]

What we do not know yet:
- [Open question]

What we are doing now:
- [Action] — [Owner]
- [Action] — [Owner]

What you should do:
- [Instruction]

Next update:
[Time and owner]
```

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Internal communication happens before or alongside external communication
- [ ] Facts are not softened into spin
- [ ] Uncertainty is explicit
- [ ] The next update has a time and owner
