# VP CPO Transition Coaching

Use this skill to coach a senior product leader through the move into VP or CPO scope. The focus is on executive accountability, alliance building, role evaluation, people-system leadership, and first-phase recalibration.

## When to Use

- A Director is preparing for VP or CPO scope
- A candidate is evaluating an executive product role before accepting
- A new VP or CPO needs to diagnose early-role friction
- An executive product leader needs to repair peer, strategy, or org-level drift

## Boundaries

- Use `collaboration/founder-leadership-coaching` for founder bottlenecks, delegation, and founder-stage leadership change.
- Use `agile-pm/board-update-decks` for board and investor communication artifacts.
- Use `workflow/strategy-execution-alignment` for OKR and goal-cascade diagnosis across teams.

## Workflow

1. **Locate the executive transition stage**
   Place the leader in one of four states:
   - preparing
   - evaluating or interviewing
   - newly landed
   - recalibrating

2. **Diagnose the executive shift**
   Look for the dominant challenge:
   - executive-level framing
   - alliance building with peers
   - people-system leadership
   - decision rights and empowerment
   - role clarity and business expectations

3. **Test the operating reality**
   If the user is pre-acceptance, turn anxiety into diligence questions. If the user is in role, separate stated expectations from how the executive team actually behaves.

4. **Translate the diagnosis into executive moves**
   Recommend concrete actions such as CEO diligence questions, peer-alignment conversations, role-boundary resets, operating-rhythm changes, or people-system interventions.

5. **End with a decision or repair plan**
   Finish with the next set of executive actions for the next 30 to 90 days.

Use `references/conversation-flow.md` when the user needs a worked example of offer-stage or early-role coaching.

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- the transition-stage diagnosis
- the main executive-risk pattern
- the decision or repair actions
- the key conversations to run next
- the 30 to 90 day focus plan

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The advice distinguishes executive scope from Director-level work
- [ ] Diligence questions are concrete enough to test role reality
- [ ] Recommendations include peer and CEO alignment, not only product craft
- [ ] The plan names the highest-risk assumptions explicitly
- [ ] The final output ends with a short decision or repair sequence
