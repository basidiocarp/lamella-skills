# Company Culture Design

Use this skill to turn culture from aspiration into operating behavior. The goal is to define what the organization rewards, tolerates, and reinforces, then express that clearly enough to hire, manage, and communicate against it.

## When to Use

- Defining or revising company values
- Building a culture code or behavioral expectations document
- Diagnosing culture drift after growth, reorgs, or hiring waves
- Designing rituals that reinforce culture rather than decorate it

## Workflow

1. **Start with observed behavior**
   Identify what the organization actually rewards, tolerates, and celebrates before rewriting values.

2. **Translate values into behaviors**
   For each value, define the visible actions, decision rules, and anti-patterns that show whether the value is real.

3. **Design the reinforcement layer**
   Decide how the culture appears in hiring, recognition, reviews, rituals, and leadership behavior.

4. **Write the culture document honestly**
   Include who thrives, who does not, how decisions get made, and how the culture is expected to evolve with scale.

5. **Add a health check**
   Define how the culture will be measured and when it will be reviewed.

Use `references/culture-playbook.md` for culture workshops, survey design, and stage-based rituals. Use `references/culture-code-template.md` when the user needs a structured culture-code draft.

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- the core culture principles
- behavior anchors for each principle
- the rituals or management systems that reinforce them
- the culture-code structure
- the review or measurement cadence

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Values are grounded in observed behavior, not slogans
- [ ] Each value has concrete behavioral anchors
- [ ] Reinforcement appears in reviews, hiring, or leadership rituals
- [ ] The document states who will and will not thrive in the environment
- [ ] Culture health has a review mechanism, not just a launch artifact
