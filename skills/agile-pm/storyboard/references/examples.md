# Storyboard Example

## Good Example

### SmartInvoice

**Frame 1: Main Character**  
Sarah is a freelance designer juggling ten client projects from a home office.

**Frame 2: Problem Emerges**  
She spends eight hours a month chasing overdue invoices instead of doing paid design work.

**Frame 3: Escalation**  
A major client is now two weeks late on a $5,000 payment, and rent is due next week.

**Frame 4: Solution Appears**  
Sarah finds an invoicing tool through a designer forum and tries the trial because the reminders are automated.

**Frame 5: Breakthrough**  
She gets a payment notification without having to make an awkward follow-up call.

**Frame 6: Life After**  
Three months later, invoicing takes thirty minutes a month and her evenings are back.

## Common Failure Modes

- the character is generic instead of specific
- the problem is abstract instead of costly
- the solution is described with features instead of outcomes
- the ending says the user is happy without proving what changed
