# Executive Onboarding Diagnostic Example

This example shows a newly hired VP Product running the first 90 days as a diagnostic process.

## Phase 0: Before Day 1

- Clarify CEO expectations for the first 90 days.
- Ask why the previous leader left.
- Confirm that the first month is for diagnosis, not visible transformation.

## Phase 1: Diagnose

- Interview executives, PMs, engineering leads, and GTM partners.
- Document recurring themes and contradictions.
- Observe decision forums before changing them.

## Phase 2: Validate

- Reality-check the emerging picture with the CEO, CRO, CFO, and CTO.
- Confirm which themes repeat across independent sources.
- Separate underused talent from role mismatch.

## Phase 3: Act with Evidence

- Publish a compact assessment of strengths, risks, and open questions.
- Rebalance only the highest-leverage priorities first.
- Start the people and alignment conversations with explicit rationale.

## Why This Example Matters

- It keeps onboarding diagnostic instead of performative.
- It shows how evidence is gathered before major decisions.
- It turns the first 90 days into a defensible operating sequence.
