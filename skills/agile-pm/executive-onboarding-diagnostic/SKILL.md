# Executive Onboarding Diagnostic

Use this skill to structure the first 90 days of an executive product transition as diagnose, validate, and then act. The goal is to avoid premature org, process, and strategy changes before the evidence base is strong enough.

## When to Use

- A new VP or CPO is entering a role and needs a first-90-days plan
- A candidate wants pre-acceptance diligence questions for an executive role
- A recently hired leader needs to validate what they are hearing before acting
- A product executive inherited a noisy organization and needs a disciplined diagnostic path

## Boundaries

- Use `agile-pm/company-operating-rhythm` after the diagnostic when designing the steady-state cadence.
- Use `collaboration/founder-leadership-coaching` for founder-specific leadership and delegation coaching.
- Use `agile-pm/board-update-decks` when the findings need to be packaged into executive or board communication.

## Workflow

1. **Phase 0: Pre-entry diligence**
   Before day 1, clarify expectations, non-negotiables, role boundaries, and the main success criteria for the first 90 to 180 days.

2. **Phase 1: Diagnose**
   Run structured interviews across executives, product, engineering, and GTM. Capture contradictions, unwritten strategy, and the difference between documented process and real behavior.

3. **Phase 2: Validate**
   Pressure-test patterns with the manager and key peers. Separate one loud opinion from repeated signal. Build the first people read, including underused talent and role mismatches.

4. **Phase 3: Act with evidence**
   Share a compact assessment, identify the three to five highest-leverage changes, and sequence actions rather than launching a full transformation.

5. **Protect the diagnostic discipline**
   Delay structural changes, role redesigns, and process overhauls until the evidence base is strong enough to defend them.

Use `references/onboarding-example.md` when the user needs a compact worked example of the 30-60-90 flow.

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- the 30-60-90 diagnostic plan
- the interview and evidence-gathering plan
- the highest-risk assumptions to validate
- the first people and strategy hypotheses
- the first three to five evidence-backed actions

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Month 1 is clearly diagnostic rather than performative action
- [ ] Unwritten strategy is treated as a discovery target
- [ ] The people assessment distinguishes hidden talent from role mismatch
- [ ] Major changes are sequenced after validation
- [ ] The final plan ends with evidence-backed actions, not a transformation slogan
