# Plan Discovery Interviews

Use this skill to prepare a discovery interview round before talking to customers, prospects, or internal stakeholders. The goal is to define what you need to learn, who you need to talk to, and how to run interviews that generate useful evidence instead of confirmation bias.

## When to Use

- Starting a new discovery research round
- Preparing for customer interviews before a product or strategy decision
- Tightening an interview plan that feels vague or overly leading
- Aligning a team on participant mix, themes, and success criteria

## Boundaries

- Use `agile-pm/discover-interview-synthesis` after the interviews are complete.
- Use `agile-pm/define-problem-statement` to sharpen the problem framing first if the research question is still fuzzy.
- Use `agile-pm/define-jtbd-canvas` after evidence starts to explain the underlying job and motivation.

## Workflow

1. **Define the research objective**
   State the decision or uncertainty the interviews need to inform. Keep it narrow enough to guide participant selection and question design.

2. **Choose the participant mix**
   Specify who should be interviewed, why they matter, and how many conversations are needed to start seeing patterns.

3. **Turn assumptions into learning goals**
   List the main hypotheses, unknowns, and risky assumptions. Use these to shape the interview guide instead of trying to prove a preferred answer.

4. **Design the interview guide**
   Build a short sequence:
   - warm-up and context
   - current behavior and workarounds
   - concrete recent examples
   - pain points and consequences
   - closing gaps and follow-ups

5. **Install bias controls**
   Remove leading phrasing, future-commitment questions, and solution pitching. Keep the interview anchored in real behavior and recent events.

6. **Define the output package**
   End with the participant plan, interview guide, note-taking structure, and criteria for whether the round answered the question.

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- the research objective
- the participant mix and rationale
- the key hypotheses or unknowns
- the interview guide
- the bias and quality checks

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The plan is tied to a real decision or uncertainty
- [ ] Participant selection is explicit and justified
- [ ] Questions focus on past behavior, not future promises
- [ ] Leading and solution-biased questions are removed
- [ ] The final plan is short enough to use in a real interview round
