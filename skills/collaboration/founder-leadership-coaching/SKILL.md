# Founder Leadership Coaching

Use this skill to coach founders through the transition from early operator to scaled leader. The focus is on practical changes in delegation, calendar design, energy management, feedback, and leadership growth.

## When to Use

- A founder feels like the bottleneck
- Delegation keeps failing or getting pulled back upward
- Calendar sprawl is crowding out strategy and recovery time
- The company has outgrown the founder's current management habits

## Boundaries

- Use `agile-pm/board-update-decks` for board and investor communication artifacts.
- Use `agile-pm/company-operating-rhythm` for company-wide operating cadence and accountability design.
- Use `agile-pm/organizational-health-diagnostic` for business health reviews rather than founder-specific coaching.

## Workflow

1. **Identify the founder pattern**
   Map the founder's strengths and recurring blind spots. Look for where the founder adds the most leverage and where they consistently create drag.

2. **Audit delegation and time use**
   Review what the founder still owns, what should move down, and what should be deleted entirely.

3. **Review energy and recovery**
   Separate energizing work, neutral work, and draining work. Protect the work only the founder should still own.

4. **Set growth priorities**
   Choose the next leadership transition to make explicit, such as hiring managers, stepping out of direct execution, or building a real executive cadence.

5. **Turn the coaching into operating changes**
   End with a short set of calendar, delegation, and behavior changes for the next quarter.

Use `references/founder-toolkit.md` for reflection prompts, delegation tools, and calendar audits. Use `references/leadership-growth.md` when the user needs stage-based leadership development guidance.

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- the founder bottleneck diagnosis
- the top delegation or calendar changes
- the main blind spots to watch
- the next-quarter leadership growth plan
- the explicit stop-doing list

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The diagnosis names specific behaviors, not personality labels
- [ ] Delegation recommendations distinguish coach / delegate / keep / kill
- [ ] Calendar changes protect strategy and recovery time
- [ ] The growth plan reflects company stage
- [ ] The advice ends with concrete operating changes, not generic encouragement
